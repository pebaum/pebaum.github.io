Inspirations & Influences

The artists, thinkers, and creators who shape my approach to generative art and creative coding.

Visual Artists & Pioneers

  • Casey Reas & Ben Fry - Creators of Processing, democratizing creative coding
  • John Whitney - Early computer graphics pioneer, analog computer art
  • Vera Molnár - Pioneer of algorithmic art, exploring randomness in systems
  • Bridget Riley - Op art master, visual perception and pattern
  • Sol LeWitt - Conceptual art instructions, systematic creativity

Contemporary Generative Artists

  • Tyler Hobbs - Fidenza and the art of controlled randomness
  • Zach Lieberman - Interactive art and human-computer expression
  • Mario Klingemann - AI art and neural network creativity
  • Memo Akten - Critical AI art and computational thinking
  • Andreas Gysin - ASCII art and terminal aesthetics

Thinkers & Writers

  • Douglas Hofstadter - Gödel, Escher, Bach: patterns and recursion
  • Stephen Wolfram - A New Kind of Science, cellular automata
  • John Conway - Game of Life, emergent complexity
  • Benoit Mandelbrot - Fractal geometry and natural patterns
  • Christopher Alexander - Pattern Language, design philosophy

Concepts & Movements

  • Emergence - Simple rules creating complex behaviors
  • Biomimicry - Learning from natural systems and processes
  • Minimal Computing - Doing more with less, constraint-based creativity
  • ASCII Aesthetics - Beauty in character-based art
  • Procedural Generation - Infinite possibilities from finite rules

Current Fascinations

What's currently capturing my attention and influencing my work:

  • Terminal-based interfaces as artistic medium
  • Procedural world generation algorithms
  • The aesthetics of command-line computing
  • Intersection of poetry and code
  • Maps as metaphors for digital exploration
