# Forward: DnD Campaign Outline

*27 Sessions across 9 Domains — Each location is 3 sessions with 3 beats each*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Total Sessions:** 27 (+ Prologue & Epilogue)  
**Level Progression:** 0 → 10  
**Party Size:** 3-5 players  

### Macro Structure

| Act | Domains | Levels | Sessions | Story Phase |
|-----|---------|--------|----------|-------------|
| **Prologue** | — | 0 | 1 | Ordinary World |
| **Act I** | Termina, Shrine, Valthria | 1-3 | 9 | Setup: Disruption → Commitment |
| **Act II** | Spiremaze, Wastes, Brume | 4-6 | 9 | Confrontation: Exploration → Crisis |
| **Act III** | Rot Glade, Gaol, Nightsea | 7-10 | 9 | Resolution: Recommitment → Climax |
| **Epilogue** | Golden City | 10+ | 1 | New Status Quo |

---

# PROLOGUE: Ordinary World
*Session 0 — Level 0*

## The World Before

**Purpose:** Establish who the characters are before everything changes.

### Moment 1: "Life in Termina"
- Players describe their characters' daily lives in Termina
- Introduce key NPCs: the protagonist's parents, village elders, childhood friends
- Establish the village's relationship with Valthria (uneasy tribute, distant overlords)

### Moment 2: "The Mirror Knight's Legacy"
- Players learn of the protagonist's father (or party patron): a former knight who retired to farming
- Hints of an old sword kept hidden, a past he doesn't speak of
- Foreshadowing: travelers speak of darkness spreading from the north

### Moment 3: "The Last Good Day"
- A village festival or harvest celebration
- Players make memories they'll carry through the campaign
- End on an ominous note: smoke on the horizon, riders approaching

**Transition:** "You wake to screaming."

# ACT I: SETUP
*Status Quo → Disruption → Commitment*

---

## DOMAIN 1: Home Village Termina
*Sessions 1-3 — Levels 0→1*

**Domain Theme:** Loss and the choice to act  
**Key Item:** Homespun Cloak, Rations  
**Domain Arc:** Witness → Grieve → Fight

---

### Session 1: "Smoke Along the Lanes"
*Beat: WITNESS — The world breaks*

#### Moment 1: "Fire in the Orchards"
- Players wake to Valthrian soldiers burning the village
- Combat tutorial: desperate fight against 2-3 soldiers (CR 1/8 guards)
- Establish that resistance is scattered, uncoordinated

#### Moment 2: "The Magistrate's Decree"
- Players witness Magistrate Ellard reading a proclamation in the square
- The protagonist's parents (or beloved NPCs) are publicly executed for "harboring sedition"
- Players must choose: intervene (and likely die) or watch (and live with it)
- **Terror Choice:** Lose HP attempting rescue, or gain a point of Exhaustion/Dread from trauma

#### Moment 3: "Cold Hearth Shadows"
- Soldiers withdraw, leaving the village in ruins
- Players search the destroyed home, find the Ancient Sword is gone (taken by Ellard)
- Discover other survivors hiding; short rest among ashes

**End Session 1:** The players are level 0 survivors with nothing but grief.

---

### Session 2: "Gravel Under Vow"
*Beat: GRIEVE — Processing loss, gathering strength*

#### Moment 1: "Keep Vigil at Their Graves"
- Funeral scene for the fallen
- Roleplaying opportunity: what do the players say? What do they promise?
- Introduce a surviving NPC who knows the manor's layout (potential guide)

#### Moment 2: "Stench of Piled Bodies"
- Players discover the mass grave at the village edge
- Ellard's cruelty revealed: this wasn't just about one family
- Stakes established: if they don't act, this continues

#### Moment 3: "Torches Gutter on Empty Streets"
- Night falls; players must decide what to do
- Gather information about Ellard's manor, his guards, his routines
- **Level Up to 1** — grief becomes determination

**End Session 2:** The players have a plan forming. They will not run.

---

### Session 3: "Liberation, But Ashes Remain"
*Beat: FIGHT — The point of no return*

#### Moment 1: "Infiltrate the Magistrate's Manor"
- Stealth/skill challenge: navigate the manor's back passages
- **Pit Challenge:** 3 successes before 3 failures (DC 12)
- Each failure: encounter turned villagers who serve Ellard out of fear
- Moral complexity: do you hurt people who are also victims?

#### Moment 2: "Confront Ellard and His Lackeys"
- Boss fight: Magistrate Ellard (CR 1 Noble) + 2 Guards
- Ellard fights dirty: hostages, traps, appeals to "the law"
- The Ancient Sword is here—but it's locked away. Do they take time to find it?
- **Victory:** Ellard dies. The players have killed an imperial official.

#### Moment 3: "Dawn Over Blackened Rows"
- Aftermath: the village cannot be saved, but it is free
- Surviving villagers will scatter; some thank the players, others blame them
- The players must leave—Valthria will send more soldiers
- **Choice presented:** Two roads forward (Forgotten Shrine or Bannered City Valthria)

**End Session 3:** Point of no return. The players are outlaws. They must go forward.

---

## DOMAIN 2: Forgotten Shrine
*Sessions 4-6 — Levels 2→3*

**Domain Theme:** Faith in darkness  
**Key Item:** Ancient Sword (required to harm the Guardian)  
**Domain Arc:** Shelter → Test → Revelation

---

### Session 4: "Providential Shelter"
*Beat: SHELTER — Finding refuge*

#### Moment 1: "Lightning Outlines Ruins"
- Players flee through a storm; lightning reveals a ruined cathedral on a hill
- Decision: shelter here or press on through dangerous weather
- The shrine radiates faint divine energy—something holy remains

#### Moment 2: "Steps Into the Nave"
- Exploration of the upper shrine: collapsed roof, shattered pews, faded murals
- Environmental storytelling: this was a major cathedral, destroyed long ago
- Discover a stair descending into darkness beneath the altar

#### Moment 3: "Trapped Hallway"
- First dungeon challenge: the lower shrine is trapped
- **Pit Challenge:** Pressure plates, falling stones, pit traps
- Introduce the concept that someone doesn't want visitors

**End Session 4:** The players have found shelter, but the shrine holds secrets below.

---

### Session 5: "Dust Wakes"
*Beat: TEST — The shrine tests the worthy*

#### Moment 1: "Supernatural Darkness"
- Descending deeper, light fails unnaturally
- Players must navigate by touch, sound, and faith
- Hint that something watches from the dark

#### Moment 2: "Assailed by Living Shadows"
- Combat: Shadow creatures (CR 1/2 Shadows, 3-4 of them)
- *"They test what the storm already found wanting"*
- Fighting in magical darkness: mechanical challenge

#### Moment 3: "Ritual Water Basin"
- Discovery: a still-functioning holy font in a hidden chamber
- Players can cleanse themselves: remove one negative condition
- Lore dump: inscriptions tell of the shrine's purpose—guarding something from the dark

**End Session 5:** The players have proven themselves to the shrine's defenses.

---

### Session 6: "Light Between Shadows"
*Beat: REVELATION — The Ancient Sword is found*

#### Moment 1: "Carvings in Pale Glow"
- The deepest chamber: walls covered in prophetic carvings
- Story revealed: the shrine was built to house a weapon against "the darkness at the edge"
- Foreshadowing: images of a dragon-like figure, three seals, companions on a journey

#### Moment 2: "The Ancient Sword"
- The players find the blade on a simple altar, untouched by time
- **The Ancient Sword:** +1 longsword, deals radiant damage, glows in darkness
- When the "worthy" player touches it, they see a vision: the Guardian, the Nightsea, the Golden City

#### Moment 3: "Candles Take and Hold"
- As players leave, candles throughout the shrine spontaneously light
- The shrine acknowledges them; they are the first in generations
- Exit to find the storm has passed; the path forward is clear

**End Session 6:** The players have the first key item. The campaign's shape becomes clear.

**Level Up to 3**

---

## DOMAIN 3: Bannered City Valthria
*Sessions 7-9 — Levels 3→4*

**Domain Theme:** Power and anonymity  
**Key Item:** Golden Bangle (required for Ghostly Princess)  
**Domain Arc:** Infiltrate → Survive → Escape

---

### Session 7: "Gate Queue Shuffles"
*Beat: INFILTRATE — Entering the enemy's heart*

#### Moment 1: "City Watch Shakedown"
- Approaching Valthria, players must pass inspection
- They're wanted criminals; how do they get in?
- Options: disguise, bribery, forged papers, sneaking, bluffing

#### Moment 2: "Vast Markets"
- Once inside: sensory overload of the imperial capital
- Opportunity to gather supplies, information, rumors
- Hear about: the empire's expansion, unrest in the provinces, a missing princess

#### Moment 3: "Papers Folded Flat"
- Players need a contact, a place to stay, a way to move unseen
- Introduction to the Valthrian underworld: thieves' guild, resistance cells
- **Hook:** Someone knows where the Golden Bangle is—a relic from the old royal family

**End Session 7:** Players have a foothold in enemy territory.

---

### Session 8: "Alleys Braid and Unbraid"
*Beat: SURVIVE — The city fights back*

#### Moment 1: "Intoxicating Spices"
- Investigation sequence: following leads through markets and taverns
- The Bangle was last seen in a noble's private collection
- Learn that an assassin has been asking about "the outlaws from Termina"

#### Moment 2: "Assassin in the Crowd"
- Combat encounter: Assassin (CR 2) ambushes the party
- Fight in a crowded market; collateral damage, fleeing civilians
- Capture or kill? The assassin knows who sent them (imperial intelligence)

#### Moment 3: "Faces Blur in the Crowd"
- Paranoia sets in: the city is watching
- Players must move safe houses, trust no one
- **Snare:** "Out of your depth" — the pressure of being hunted

**End Session 8:** The walls are closing in. They need to get what they came for and leave.

---

### Session 9: "Humility"
*Beat: ESCAPE — Taking what they need and getting out*

#### Moment 1: "Coins Click Like Rain"
- Heist sequence: infiltrating the noble's manor
- Stealth, deception, or violence—player choice
- The Golden Bangle is in a display case, along with other relics

#### Moment 2: "Golden Bangle"
- Players acquire the bangle: *"Warmth remembered in gold"*
- It seems to have no magical properties... yet
- Alarm raised; chase sequence through the manor

#### Moment 3: "Gutter Water Finds a Drain"
- Escape through the city's underbelly: sewers, tunnels, rooftops
- Final obstacle: City Watch blockade at the gate
- Players must choose their exit route—this determines their next domain

**End Session 9:** Players escape Valthria with the Golden Bangle. Act I complete.

**Level Up to 4**

**Transition to Act II:** The players have struck at the empire and survived. Now they must find allies.

---

# ACT II: CONFRONTATION
*Exploration → Midpoint → Fall and Crisis*

---

## DOMAIN 4: The Caelith Spiremaze
*Sessions 10-12 — Levels 4→5*

**Domain Theme:** Questions and identity  
**Key Items:** Mirror Shard, Ghostly Princess (companion)  
**Domain Arc:** Ascend → Encounter → Choose

---

### Session 10: "Labyrinthine Ascent"
*Beat: ASCEND — Entering the maze of questions*

#### Moment 1: "Spire Upon Spire"
- The Spiremaze appears as impossible architecture: towers that defy geometry
- Players must find a way in; the obvious entrance is an illusion
- Environmental puzzle: finding the real door

#### Moment 2: "Stairs That Disagree"
- Inside: Escher-like navigation challenges
- Skill challenge: each player contributes to finding the path
- Glimpses of a figure moving in parallel—always just out of sight

#### Moment 3: "Psychic Malaise of Questions"
- The maze itself asks questions: "Why do you continue?" "What have you lost?"
- Players must answer honestly or face psychic damage
- Introduce the theme: this place tests identity, not strength

**End Session 10:** Players have entered the Spiremaze. They are not alone.

---

### Session 11: "The Damsel in a Distant Tower"
*Beat: ENCOUNTER — Meeting the Ghostly Princess*

#### Moment 1: "Constellation Shaped Like a Sword"
- Navigation by starlight in impossible spaces
- Players discover the Mirror Shard on a pedestal
- **Mirror Shard:** *"It shows the cut between who you are and could be"*

#### Moment 2: "High Light"
- At the maze's heart: a tower containing a ghostly figure
- The Ghostly Princess: trapped between worlds, fading
- She speaks of being betrayed, forgotten, waiting for someone to remember her

#### Moment 3: "The Bangle Remembers Her Pulse"
- If players have the Golden Bangle, they can anchor her
- **Terror Choice:** Give the bangle (she follows) or keep it (she fades forever)
- The princess's knowledge: she knows of the Guardian, the seals, her father the Mirror Knight

**End Session 11:** Players have a choice that defines their path.

---

### Session 12: "Purpose: Follow and Protect"
*Beat: CHOOSE — The princess joins or is lost*

#### Moment 1: "Footfalls Find a Second Rhythm"
- If the princess joins: she guides them through the remaining maze
- If she doesn't: the maze becomes hostile, players must fight their way out
- Either way: approach the exit

#### Moment 2: "Scholarly Sphinx at the Exit"
- Guardian of the exit: a sphinx demands answers, not combat
- Riddle challenge: answers require understanding what they've learned
- The sphinx speaks of the Mirror Knight: *"He sought answers too. He found only reflection."*

#### Moment 3: "Door That Was Not There"
- Exit the Spiremaze changed
- The princess (if present) speaks of her father—last seen in the Sirocco Wastes
- **Blessing:** "Purpose: follow and protect" — the party has direction

**End Session 12:** The midpoint approaches. The Mirror Knight awaits.

**Level Up to 5**

---

## DOMAIN 5: Sirocco Wastes
*Sessions 13-15 — Levels 5→6*

**Domain Theme:** Self-confrontation  
**Key Item:** Mirror Knight (companion, breaks Seal of Acedia)  
**Domain Arc:** Endure → Confront → Recognize

---

### Session 13: "Four Days Under the Red Sun"
*Beat: ENDURE — The desert strips away pretense*

#### Moment 1: "Heat Shimmer Road"
- Enter the wastes: a dead empire's bones baking in endless sun
- Survival mechanics: exhaustion, water rationing, navigation
- Find scattered adventuring gear, ruined: others have tried this path

#### Moment 2: "Sand-Eaten War Colossus"
- Discovery: massive statues half-buried, remnants of a fallen civilization
- Environmental storytelling: this was an empire that challenged something and lost
- Shelter in the colossus's shadow; rest encounter

#### Moment 3: "Desiccating Winds"
- Sandstorm forces the party to hunker down
- Visions in the sand: each player sees something from their past
- **Snare potential:** "Featureless expanse" — the desert erodes certainty

**End Session 13:** The party is weakened but pushing forward. Something follows them.

---

### Session 14: "Long Shadow Keeps Pace"
*Beat: CONFRONT — The Mirror Knight appears*

#### Moment 1: "Counting to the Next Shade"
- Players realize they're being followed
- A figure that matches their pace exactly, always at the edge of vision
- Attempts to approach or flee fail—the distance stays constant

#### Moment 2: "The Mirror Knight"
- The figure finally approaches: a knight in reflective armor
- He attacks without words; his fighting style mirrors the party's
- **Unwinnable Fight:** His HP equals combined party HP; he copies all attacks

#### Moment 3: "A Reflection That Cannot Be Defeated"
- Combat continues until players realize they can't win by force
- If they have the Mirror Shard: using it shows the knight his true face
- **Recognition:** He stops. He remembers. He is the protagonist's father (or the princess's father, or a legendary hero)

**End Session 14:** The Midpoint Revelation. A lost ally returns.

---

### Session 15: "Gratitude"
*Beat: RECOGNIZE — The Mirror Knight joins; stakes revealed*

#### Moment 1: "The Knight's Story"
- The Mirror Knight explains: he challenged the Guardian and was cursed
- He's been wandering as a reflection, unable to die or live
- With his identity restored, he can fight again—but only if he reaches the Guardian

#### Moment 2: "The Three Seals"
- The Knight knows the Guardian's weakness: three seals protect it
- **Seal of Aporia** (Ignorance): Cannot be reached without the Sage's guidance
- **Seal of Acedia** (Sloth): Regenerates unless the Mirror Knight is present
- **Seal of Agnosia** (Unknowing): Reflects damage unless the Princess is present
- The party realizes: they need all three companions, or the fight is unwinnable

#### Moment 3: "Green Rumor at the Edge"
- The desert ends; trees appear on the horizon
- The Mirror Knight's blessing: *"Gratitude — empty hands, lighter heart"*
- Full heal; snares cleansed; the party is restored

**End Session 15:** Stakes are fully raised. The party knows what they need. Act II's second half begins.

**Level Up to 6**

---

## DOMAIN 6: Darkwood Brume
*Sessions 16-18 — Levels 6→7*

**Domain Theme:** Direction in despair  
**Key Item:** The Sage (companion, breaks Seal of Aporia with Ferryman's Bell)  
**Domain Arc:** Lost → Found → Guided

---

### Session 16: "Mist and Misdirection"
*Beat: LOST — The forest erases purpose*

#### Moment 1: "Brook in the Shade"
- Enter the Darkwood: mist so thick light becomes suggestion
- Initial exploration: paths that loop, landmarks that move
- Find a brook—following it seems logical but leads nowhere

#### Moment 2: "False Trails and Lost Purpose"
- **Pit Challenge:** Navigate the forest (DC 15, 3 successes before 3 failures)
- Each failure: the party loses time, gains exhaustion, hears whispers of doubt
- The forest asks: *"Why bother? The world is already lost."*

#### Moment 3: "Marks Blur to Nothing"
- Trail markers the party left have vanished
- The Mirror Knight grows silent; the Princess flickers
- **Snare:** "Why bother" — mechanical penalty to all checks from creeping despair

**End Session 16:** The party is truly lost. Morale breaks.

---

### Session 17: "Footsteps Ahead"
*Beat: FOUND — A guide appears*

#### Moment 1: "Light Thickens Then Thins"
- At their lowest point, a light appears in the mist
- Following it: a small campfire, a robed figure waiting
- The Sage: *"A robed figure steps from the mist. He nods once."*

#### Moment 2: "The Sage's Silence"
- The Sage doesn't speak—communicates through gestures, writing, expressions
- He knows the way. He's been waiting.
- Campfire scene: rest, recovery, the Sage shares food and warmth

#### Moment 3: "Trail Brightens by Degrees"
- With the Sage leading, the forest's hostility fades
- The path becomes clear—not easier, but possible
- The Sage shows them: they need the Ferryman's Bell (found in the Nightsea)

**End Session 17:** Hope returns. The party has their third companion.

---

### Session 18: "Guidance"
*Beat: GUIDED — Emerging from despair*

#### Moment 1: "Fog Blinks First"
- The Sage leads them through the last of the forest
- Combat encounter: Mist-Spun Giant Spider (CR 4)—the forest's final test
- Fight in limited visibility; trust the Sage's guidance

#### Moment 2: "Sunstone"
- Discovery: the Sunstone, an item that creates light even in magical darkness
- The Sage gives it to the party—he's carried it for this moment
- *"You'll need this where you're going."*

#### Moment 3: "Guidance"
- Exit the Darkwood; clear sky for the first time in sessions
- **Blessing:** "Guidance" — cleanse snare, bonus to next challenge
- The path forward splits: Amara Rot Glade or Frozen Gaol

**End Session 18:** Act II complete. The dark night of the soul approaches.

**Level Up to 7**

---

# ACT III: RESOLUTION
*Recommitment → Approach → Climax*

---

## DOMAIN 7: Amara Rot Glade
*Sessions 19-21 — Levels 7→8*

**Domain Theme:** Mercy in a merciless world  
**Key Item:** Bleeding Heart Talisman  
**Domain Arc:** Disgust → Betrayal → Mercy

---

### Session 19: "Tar Pits and Crimson Vines"
*Beat: DISGUST — A place of rot and resentment*

#### Moment 1: "Resentment Blooms"
- Enter the Rot Glade: swamp of red plants, biting insects, decay
- The land itself feels hostile—not evil, but hurt
- Navigation challenge: finding solid ground

#### Moment 2: "Thorns Watching"
- The party realizes they're being observed
- Shapes in the foliage: humanoid, but wrong
- Encounter: hostile survivors who've adapted to the rot—they attack strangers

#### Moment 3: "Step Lifts"
- Discovery: the rot isn't natural. Something poisoned this place.
- Find evidence of a settlement destroyed by betrayal
- **Snare potential:** "Eyes on the banks" — paranoia from being watched

**End Session 19:** The glade hides a story of betrayal.

---

### Session 20: "Betrayal Shock"
*Beat: BETRAYAL — Trust is tested*

#### Moment 1: "Drowning Ambush"
- Combat: Gravecoil Ambusher (CR 5)—death that waits with patience
- Fight in difficult terrain; the swamp itself is an enemy
- One party member is separated/captured during the fight

#### Moment 2: "Still Water Listens"
- The captured character is brought before the glade's "queen"
- A woman transformed by rot, bitter with rage
- She offers a deal: betray your companions, and I'll let you live

#### Moment 3: "Mud Rinsed from Hands"
- The party must rescue their companion or trust them to resist
- Confrontation with the queen—she reveals her story: betrayed by those she trusted
- **Terror Choice:** Fight her (violence) or show her mercy (risk)

**End Session 20:** The theme crystallizes: does the party perpetuate the cycle?

---

### Session 21: "Lay Her to Rest"
*Beat: MERCY — Breaking the cycle*

#### Moment 1: "Air Sweetens Toward Mercy"
- If mercy shown: the queen's rage breaks. She weeps.
- She asks to be released—not saved, just allowed to die
- If violence: the glade turns fully hostile; fight through to escape

#### Moment 2: "Pity for the Fallen"
- Burial scene: laying the queen to rest with dignity
- The rot begins to recede—not healed, but no longer spreading
- **Rest:** "Lay her to rest" — the party processes what they've done

#### Moment 3: "Rot Offers Passage"
- The glade opens a path: it recognizes those who chose mercy
- **Blessing:** Bleeding Heart Talisman — resistance to poison, symbol of compassion
- Exit toward the Frozen Gaol

**End Session 21:** The party has proven they can show mercy. This matters for what comes next.

**Level Up to 8**

---

## DOMAIN 8: Frozen Gaol
*Sessions 22-24 — Levels 8→9*

**Domain Theme:** Freedom against judgment  
**Key Item:** Warden's Ice Shield  
**Domain Arc:** Imprisonment → Judgment → Escape

---

### Session 22: "Fracturing Ice Floor"
*Beat: IMPRISONMENT — Trapped in a frozen hell*

#### Moment 1: "Cold Ward of Silence"
- Enter the Frozen Gaol: a prison built into a glacier
- Immediately, the party is separated—each in their own cell
- Solo scenes: each character faces isolation

#### Moment 2: "Panopticon Isolation"
- The prison's design: everyone can be watched, no one can connect
- **Snare:** "Panopticon isolation" — -1 to all rolls while watched
- Players must find ways to communicate across cells

#### Moment 3: "Crack Lines Underfoot"
- Discovery: the prison is old, crumbling, the ice fractured
- A way to escape the cells—but it means going deeper, not out
- The party reunites; companions are shaken but intact

**End Session 22:** The party is together but still trapped.

---

### Session 23: "Life Under Judgment"
*Beat: JUDGMENT — Facing Thyra the Warden*

#### Moment 1: "Judgment Like a Draft"
- Moving through the prison: frozen prisoners, eternal sentences
- Lore: this prison was built to hold the empire's enemies forever
- Many prisoners were innocents; some were genuine monsters

#### Moment 2: "No Gaze Finds You"
- The party reaches the warden's sanctum
- **Thyra the Warden:** She's been alone for centuries, frozen in duty
- She demands the party submit to judgment—everyone has crimes

#### Moment 3: "Thyra, the Warden"
- Boss fight: Thyra (CR 7) — ice magic, chains, relentless judgment
- Her combat table: Ram, Shield, Stasis Chain, Rime Blast, Ice Spear, Impale
- The fight tests everything the party has learned

**End Session 23:** Defeat Thyra or convince her to release them.

---

### Session 24: "Melted Way Out"
*Beat: ESCAPE — Freedom earned*

#### Moment 1: "Bridge Whitening with Rime"
- Post-battle: Thyra's defeat (or change of heart) opens a path
- But the prison is collapsing—the party must move fast
- Action sequence: navigating crumbling ice bridges

#### Moment 2: "Great White Wolf"
- Final obstacle: a creature that guards the exit
- Combat or diplomacy—the wolf can be reasoned with if approached right
- It was Thyra's companion once; it mourns her or follows her release

#### Moment 3: "Keys Ring Somewhere Far"
- Exit the Gaol; the prison collapses behind them
- **Blessing:** "Melted way out" — cleanse snare, full heal
- The Nightsea Expanse lies ahead—the final domain before the Guardian

**End Session 24:** The party is as strong as they'll ever be. The end approaches.

**Level Up to 9**

---

## DOMAIN 9: The Nightsea Expanse
*Sessions 25-27 — Levels 9→10*

**Domain Theme:** Acceptance and identity  
**Key Item:** Ferryman's Bell (required for crossing), Pearl of Opal Dawn  
**Domain Arc:** Descent → Crossing → Arrival

---

### Session 25: "Find the Underground River"
*Beat: DESCENT — Into the final darkness*

#### Moment 1: "A Cavern River Leads Deeper"
- The Nightsea is underground: a vast cave system containing a lightless ocean
- The party must find the river that leads to the sea
- Environmental challenge: navigation in total darkness (Sunstone helps)

#### Moment 2: "Boat Skims the Dark"
- Discovery: an ancient boat, waiting at the river's edge
- The Sage rings a bell—the Ferryman's Bell, found here
- The boat responds; it will carry them, but only forward

#### Moment 3: "Eroding Sense of Self"
- As they sail, identity begins to blur
- **Snare:** "Eroding sense of self" — -1 to all rolls
- The sea tests whether they know who they are

**End Session 25:** The final journey begins.

---

### Session 26: "The Dark Night of the Soul"
*Beat: CROSSING — Testing everything*

#### Moment 1: "Horizon is a Sound"
- Sailing in absolute darkness; time loses meaning
- The party must lean on each other—companion scenes
- The Mirror Knight speaks of his failure; the Princess speaks of her death; the Sage writes of his waiting

#### Moment 2: "Austere Leviathan"
- Boss encounter: a sea creature rises from the deep (CR 8)
- Combat on the boat; risk of capsizing
- The leviathan tests whether they deserve to pass

#### Moment 3: "Aeron the Half-Changed"
- After the leviathan: another boat approaches
- Aeron: a man trapped mid-transformation, neither human nor monster
- **Terror Choice:** End his suffering (violence) or help him complete the change (risk)

**End Session 26:** The far shore approaches. One more trial.

---

### Session 27: "Acceptance"
*Beat: ARRIVAL — Ready for the final battle*

#### Moment 1: "Water Decides Twice"
- Final obstacle: a whirlpool that asks a question
- *"What drove you here?"* — each player must answer honestly
- Wrong answers: the boat circles endlessly. Right answers: passage granted.

#### Moment 2: "Phosphor Wakes in the Wake"
- The far shore appears: bioluminescence lights the way
- The party lands; ahead, the Guardian's domain rises
- **Blessing:** "Acceptance" — cleanse all snares, full heal

#### Moment 3: "You Sail On Without Guarantees"
- Final preparation: inventory check, companion status, seal requirements
- The party confirms what they have:
  - Ancient Sword? ✓ (can damage Guardian)
  - Sage + Ferryman's Bell? ✓ (crossed the Nightsea, Seal of Aporia broken)
  - Mirror Knight? ✓ (Seal of Acedia broken — Guardian can't regenerate)
  - Ghostly Princess? ✓ (Seal of Agnosia broken — damage isn't reflected)
- The Guardian awaits

**End Session 27:** The party is ready. The climax begins.

**Level Up to 10**

# EPILOGUE: The Golden City

## The Guardian
*Final Boss Session — Level 10*

### Moment 1: "Rock Rises"
- The Guardian's domain: a island of stone in the underground sea
- Approach sequence: the Guardian manifests as they climb
- Description: a dragon of shadow and chaos, twenty feet of ancient malice

### Moment 2: "The Dragon of Chaos"
- Final boss fight: The Guardian
- **Stats:** 200+ HP, Legendary Actions, Lair Actions
- **Combat Table:**
  - Eclipse Roar (-1 to next player roll)
  - Shadow Gale (damage + skip next turn)
  - Gloaming Wave (damage + no rerolls)
  - Corona Radiation (heavy damage)
  - Devour (heavy damage, ignores blocks)
  - Black Zenith (block + heavy damage)
- Without the three seals broken, the fight is unwinnable

### Moment 3: "Forward to the Golden City"
- The Guardian falls; twilight breaks
- Light—real sunlight—floods the underground sea
- The Golden City is revealed: not a place, but a possibility. The world without the Guardian's shadow.
